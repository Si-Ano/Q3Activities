Fire

Data reflect wildland fires and acres burned nationwide, including wildland fires on federal and nonfederal lands. From 2013 to 2022, there were an average of 61,410 wildfires annually and an average of 7.2 million acres impacted annually. In 2022, 68,988 wildfires burned 7.6 million acres. An estimated 358,500 home fires occur every year. 50% of these fires start in the kitchen, 7% begin in the bedroom, and 6% are chimney fires, 4% of all residential home fires start in the living room, while 3% start from the laundry room.


Did you know that if a fire starts in your home you may have as little as two minutes to escape? During a fire, early warning from a working smoke alarm plus a fire escape plan that has been practiced regularly can save lives. Learn what else to do to keep your loved ones safe!

What measures should we do to increase fire safety?

Here are some few ways

Keep children, pets, and flammable items at least three feet away from burners
Never leave a fire (especially the ones in a fireplace) unattended
Unplug items when going out of the room
Promote the use of surge protectors in your house
Do not smoke at home
Minimize the clutter in your house and keep the debris down
Install smoke alarms in every room in the house
Talk to your family about a fire escape plan
If a fire occurs, immediately go out and call for help

Fun Fact: March is observed as the Fire Prevention Month to remind the public the importance of Fire Safety.


What might happen if these measures are not taken seriously?

Chances of a fire increase and...

  • You and your family are at risk for injuries, and maybe even death
  • Damage to property will evidently arise
  • Damage to nature will proceed increasing air and land pollution
